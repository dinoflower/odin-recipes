Fhloston Fhluffs

Description

These delicate cookies will make you feel like you've won a trip to paradise.

There's no need to be indimidated by meringues! With a few simple ingredients and a little patience, you'll be super-green.

Ingredients

  • two egg whites, ideally at room temperature
  • 1/4 tsp cream of tartar
  • pinch of salt
  • 1/2 cup + 1 Tbsp granulated sugar, ideally superfine
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract or other flavoring, optional
  • blue (or any other color!) gel food coloring, optional

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 200Â°F (be mindful if your oven runs hot) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. (Tip: Parchment paper helps to absorb excess moisture!)
  2. In a large metal bowl, beat the egg whites with the cream of tartar and the salt until soft peaks form. You'll probably want to use an electric mixer.
  3. Gradually add the sugar and continue to beat until stiff and glossy.
  4. If using vanilla or another flavoring, fold it in now.
  5. If using food coloring, paint stripes of it onto the inside of your piping bag or swirl it into the meringue to create random patterns that will pop pop pop!
  6. Pipe meringues onto your prepared baking sheet. (If you don't have a pastry bag, you can use a spoon.)
  7. Bake for 1 hour and 30 minutes. Don't open the oven too early! That said, if you like a chewy texture, set a timer for 20 minutes early to check for doneness.
  8. When the meringues are finished baking, turn off the oven and leave them inside, with the door closed, for three hours. Longer (or overnight!) works perfectly as well.
